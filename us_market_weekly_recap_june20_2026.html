Macro Themes of the Week
BULLISH US–Iran Peace Deal — Strait of Hormuz Reopened

A preliminary peace agreement announced June 15 triggered one of the year's biggest single-day rallies. Iran agreed to allow free shipping through the Strait of Hormuz; the US ended its blockade of Iranian oil exports. Brent crude immediately fell ~4.8% to ~$83/bbl, a three-month low, as markets priced in 1.5–2M additional barrels/day returning to supply.

CAUTION Fed Holds Rates — But Dot Plot Turns Hawkish

In Kevin Warsh's inaugural FOMC meeting, the Fed held the fed funds rate steady at 3.50%–3.75% for the fourth consecutive meeting. However, the updated dot plot shocked markets: the median official now expects rates to end 2026 higher than today, a reversal from March's implied cut. 9 of 19 officials backed continued rate hikes this year. Markets now price a 25bp hike as early as October 2026.
